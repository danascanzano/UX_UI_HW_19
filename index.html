hi, my name is dana...

UX/UI design student based in Oakville, ON looking for a designer role with room to grow. Passionate about developing digital solutions to improve the lives of consumers and bring beauty to the every day. Strengths in creativity, teamwork, and building projects from ideation to execution. Advertising background coupled with strong user research skills and an eye for detail.

  • Born and raised in Toronto but spent time living and working abroad (Australia, Japan, Montréal)
  • I’ve always enjoyed creating things. When I was little I would write and star in plays, draw maps of make-believe towns and create personalities for my stuffed animals (aka. Proto Personas). In my adult years, I dabbled in photography, blogging, took a certificate in graphic design and worked in content creation when social media platforms were up and coming.
  • Spent 5 years working in advertising but wasn’t as involved in the creative process as I would’ve liked to be. I did play a part in creating some cool commercials for brands like Command, Redken and Pampers.

my UX Skills

Affinity Diagrams

Used to organize a large number of ideas from a brainstorming sessions. Part of the Research & Definition phase to process, sort, and identify insights in data.

User Personas

Based on user and web research, used to create realistic representations of key user segments. User Personas ground any project on a target audience so designers know who exactly they are designing for.

User Testing

Following Dan Norman's philosophy, User Tests were executed to test prototypes amongst random and sometimes not random users. Insights are used from tests to iterate on prototypes.

my Work

Road App

Our mobile solution that encouraged and offered exercise and community connection for people who experienced isolation, particularly those who worked from home during the pandemic. Users can learn about local parks, trails and other outdoor resources through the app and enjoy them as a group as a way to safely connect with others through a shared mutual experience.

Mississauga Humane Society Website Redesign

The team observed that users are routinely confused with the repetitive features and unclear methods to support the organization, leading to our redesign of the MHS website. Our solution included simplifying the navigation and removing unnecessary, repetitive links to create a universally cohesive visual.

Canada Post Website Redesign

Canada Post website lacks an understandable hierarchy and has a lot of duplication across its features & tools, making it difficult for users to navigate around. Our team proposed a redesign of the navigation to better help users to find what they need and reduce confusion with features.
